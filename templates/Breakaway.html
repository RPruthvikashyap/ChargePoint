Breakaway Fault Diagnostic

Understanding Breakaway Faults

Breakaway faults occur when a charging cord is pulled with enough force to move the wires internally. This action opens a protective switch within the station, indicating possible damage to the cable or its connection.

Step 1: Initial Assessment of the Breakaway Fault

A breakaway fault can indicate that the charging cable has been cut or forcibly pulled out of the station.

  • Have the site contact **identify the extent of the damage**.
  • **Note:** There will be times where there is no visible external damage, but the breakaway fault persists due to an internal issue.
  • **Obtain pictures** of the station/cable showing the damage (or lack thereof) for documentation.

Action: Proceed with Replacement Options

Given the breakaway fault, the cable and/or internal components are compromised. Proceed with the appropriate replacement options:

  • If the charging cable has been identified as cut or pulled out due to the breakaway event, a **cord repair/replacement** is typically required.
  • Even if no further *external* damage beyond the breakaway cable is identified, but the fault persists, it indicates an internal issue with the cable or its connection that necessitates **cord repair/replacement**.
  • For severe internal damage not limited to the cord (e.g., internal wiring to the head unit), a **full head unit replacement** might be necessary, depending on the station model and extent of damage.
  • Quote for **cord repair** if only the cable is affected.
